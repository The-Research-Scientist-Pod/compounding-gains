Breakfast PSMF Training Under 10 min
Egg & cottage cheese scramble
Two whole eggs scrambled gently with 0% cottage cheese mixed in, finished with chives. Sautéed spinach and cherry tomatoes on the side.
340kcal
48gprotein
8gcarbs
11gfat

Ingredients

  • 2 whole eggs
  • 200g 0% fat cottage cheese
  • 100g spinach
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Handful cherry tomatoes
  • Fresh chives, salt, black pepper

Method

Whisk eggs with salt and pepper. Heat pan on low-medium. Pour eggs in, stir gently with spatula. When eggs are 70% set, fold in cottage cheese. Remove from heat while still creamy. Sauté spinach in same pan with garlic for 60 seconds. Plate with tomatoes and chives.

Why it works

Two-phase amino acid profile from fast-digesting egg protein and slow casein. Yolks deliver the day's choline, fat-soluble vitamins, and the small amount of dietary fat needed for hormonal support without pushing the meal into high-fat territory.

Breakfast PSMF Under 10 min
Greek yoghurt protein bowl
Skyr or Greek 0%, scoop of whey, frozen berries, cinnamon, optional crushed walnuts on training days only.
310kcal
50gprotein
15gcarbs
2gfat

Ingredients

  • 250g 0% Greek yoghurt or skyr
  • 1 scoop whey protein isolate (25–30g)
  • 80g frozen mixed berries
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Vanilla extract (optional)

Method

Mix yoghurt and whey thoroughly with a fork until smooth. Add berries straight from frozen — they thaw and release juice into the yoghurt for a swirl effect. Dust with cinnamon. Eat slowly.

Why it works

Dessert-coded but functionally a casein-heavy protein anchor. Berries provide antioxidants and the slow-release sugars are minimal enough to keep insulin response controlled. Cinnamon supports glucose handling.

Breakfast Training
Oats, whey, berries, electrolytes
Pre-training carb anchor with sustained protein. Best 90 minutes before resistance work.
420kcal
38gprotein
55gcarbs
6gfat

Ingredients

  • 60g rolled oats
  • 1 scoop whey protein isolate
  • 100g 0% Greek yoghurt
  • 80g mixed berries
  • Cinnamon, pinch of salt
  • Electrolyte serving (separate)

Method

Cook oats with water and a pinch of salt. Once cooked, fold in whey (mix vigorously to avoid clumping) and yoghurt. Top with berries and cinnamon. Drink electrolytes alongside.

Why it works

Oats provide sustained-release carbs that prime glycogen for training without spiking insulin too sharply. Whey delivers fast amino acids; yoghurt extends the protein release. This is the training-day anchor — not a PSMF meal.

Lunch PSMF Training
Chicken with broccoli & kimchi
Pan-grilled chicken breast, garlic broccoli or tenderstem, kimchi, sliced radishes. The PSMF workhorse.
270kcal
48gprotein
10gcarbs
3gfat

Ingredients

  • 180g chicken breast
  • 200g broccoli or tenderstem
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 100g kimchi
  • 50g sliced radishes
  • Paprika, salt, lemon

Method

Season chicken with paprika and salt. Pan-grill 4–5 minutes each side until cooked through. Rest. Meanwhile, steam broccoli 4 minutes, then finish in the hot pan with sliced garlic for 60 seconds. Plate with kimchi and radishes, lemon wedge.

Why it works

Brassicas are PSMF royalty — high volume, low calorie, sulforaphane and DIM precursors, hold texture well. Chicken breast delivers 48g of protein for under 300 kcal. Kimchi provides the satiety hit and microbiome support.

Dinner PSMF Training
White fish with sweet potato & greens
Cod or haddock baked with herbs, roasted sweet potato (the one carb meal of a PSMF day), asparagus, large salad.
330kcal
46gprotein
25gcarbs
1gfat

Ingredients

  • 180g cod or haddock fillet
  • 100g sweet potato
  • 200g asparagus
  • Mixed lettuce salad (different types)
  • Lemon, herbs, paprika
  • Vinegar-based dressing (no oil)

Method

Cube sweet potato, toss with paprika and salt, roast 25 min at 200°C. Last 10 minutes, add fish in foil with lemon and herbs to the oven. Steam asparagus in last 4 minutes. Plate with large salad and vinegar dressing.

Why it works

Sweet potato is the one meaningful carb intake on a PSMF day — supports tryptophan transport for sleep, primes glycogen for next morning, prevents the sleep-disrupting effects of fully zero-carb days. White fish is the lowest-fat protein source available, easy to digest before bed.

Dinner PSMF Training
Lean steak with asparagus & salad
5%-fat lean steak, charred asparagus, peppery leaves, mustard-vinegar dressing. The PSMF "indulgence."
360kcal
52gprotein
8gcarbs
12gfat

Ingredients

  • 200g extra-lean steak (5% fat or lower)
  • 200g asparagus
  • Mixed leaves (rocket, watercress)
  • Dijon mustard, red wine vinegar
  • Garlic, black pepper, sea salt

Method

Rub steak with garlic, salt, and pepper. Heat pan very hot. Sear steak 2 min each side for medium-rare. Rest 5 min. Char asparagus in same pan. Whisk dressing from mustard and vinegar (no oil). Slice steak across the grain over salad and asparagus.

Why it works

The fat content is higher than other PSMF dinners but the protein density and the psychological satisfaction of a "real meal" makes it worth the trade. Lean cuts (5% fat eye round, sirloin, top side) keep total fat manageable while delivering iron, B12, and creatine that supplemental sources don't match.

Dinner PSMF
Prawn stir-fry with brassicas
Cooked prawns, broccoli, bok choy, peppers, garlic, ginger, soy and chilli. Asian-inflected, very low calorie.
280kcal
42gprotein
15gcarbs
3gfat

Ingredients

  • 200g cooked king prawns
  • 200g broccoli
  • 100g bok choy or pak choi
  • 1 red pepper
  • Garlic, ginger, fresh chilli
  • Light soy sauce, rice vinegar, lime
  • Spring onions, coriander

Method

Heat pan or wok very hot with a splash of water (no oil). Add garlic, ginger, chilli for 30 seconds. Add broccoli and pepper, stir-fry 3 minutes. Add bok choy and prawns, toss briefly to warm through. Finish with soy, rice vinegar, and lime. Top with spring onion and coriander.

Why it works

Stir-frying with water and high heat instead of oil keeps the fat budget intact while preserving texture. Prawns are one of the lowest-fat animal proteins available. The brassica combination plus fresh ginger and chilli has anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Breakfast PSMF Under 10 min
Egg white omelette with feta & spinach
Fluffy egg white omelette with a controlled hit of feta, wilted spinach, tomatoes, and herbs. Ultra-low fat alternative to the whole-egg breakfast.
250kcal
42gprotein
8gcarbs
5gfat

Ingredients

  • 6 egg whites (or 200ml liquid whites)
  • 30g feta cheese
  • 100g spinach
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Fresh basil or oregano
  • Garlic, salt, black pepper

Method

Whisk whites with salt, pepper, and a splash of water until slightly foamy. Heat non-stick pan low-medium. Pour whites in, swirl. As they set, scatter wilted spinach, crumbled feta, and tomatoes on one half. Fold and slide onto plate. Top with herbs.

Why it works

The whole-egg breakfast brings 10g fat from yolks — fine most days but not always wanted. This swap keeps protein high while cutting the fat budget for days when later meals will use it (e.g. salmon dinner, smoked mackerel lunch). Feta provides the savoury hit and calcium without much fat.

36gprotein
18gcarbs
12gfat

Ingredients

  • 200g broccoli

Dinner PSMF
Beef carpaccio-style with rocket

320kcal
48gprotein
4gcarbs
12gfat

Ingredients

  • 180g lean beef fillet or sirloin

  • 30g shaved parmesan (optional)
  • Dijon mustard, lemon, balsamic vinegar

Drain most of the oil from the tuna (keep about 1 tsp). Arrange on a plate with olives, pickles, tomatoes. Squeeze lemon, scatter oregano and pepper.

Mediterranean-coded snack that feels indulgent because of the olives and the small amount of olive oil clinging to the tuna. The fat content is from monounsaturates which keep you satisfied without crashing energy. Five-minute assembly, no cooking, travels well.
